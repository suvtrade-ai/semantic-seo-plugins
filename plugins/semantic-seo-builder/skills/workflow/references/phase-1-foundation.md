# Phase 1 — Foundation & Strategy

## Goal
Establish the market context before touching any content or technical decisions. Every subsequent phase depends on the clarity built here.

## Market Research

Search for the following and synthesize findings:
- Top 10 ranking sites for the primary service + city query
- What services appear most prominently across competitors
- What trust signals and credentials dominate the niche (certifications, awards, years in business)
- Price range signals (if visible in search results or structured data)

## Market Segmentation

Divide the market into 4–6 distinct segments based on:
- Customer type (residential vs. commercial, age group, gender if relevant)
- Purchase stage (awareness, consideration, decision)
- Geography (hyper-local vs. broader metro area)
- Service need (urgent/emergency vs. planned)

Each segment gets a row in a table: Segment Name | Description | Primary Search Behavior | Value to Business

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Construction

Build 2–3 ICPs. For each:
- Name (fictional but realistic)
- Demographics
- Problem they are searching to solve
- Queries they would type (include exact phrasing)
- What would make them convert (trust signals, price transparency, speed of response)
- Which pages they would land on first

## Search Intent Mapping

For this business niche + geography, map queries across 4 intent types:

| Intent Type | Definition | Example Queries | Page Type to Target |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Informational | Learning about the topic | "how to...", "what is..." | Blog/supporting content |
| Commercial | Comparing options | "best X in Y", "X vs Y" | Category/comparison pages |
| Transactional | Ready to hire/buy | "X near me", "hire X", "X cost" | Service pages, homepage |
| Navigational | Looking for specific brand | Brand name queries | Homepage, about page |

## Competitor Gap Analysis

For each of the 2–3 competitors provided:

**Semantic gaps**: Topics the competitor does NOT cover that you can own
**Topical gaps**: Subtopics within covered areas that are thin or missing
**Entity gaps**: Named entities (locations, certifications, tools, people) the competitor doesn't mention
**Content quality gaps**: Where competitor content is shallow, outdated, or mismatched to intent

Present as a table: Competitor | Semantic Gap | Topical Gap | Entity Gap | Opportunity Rating (H/M/L)

## Output format

Write the `01-foundation.md` file with clear H2 sections for each area above. Use tables wherever possible. Be specific to the actual business — no generic examples.
